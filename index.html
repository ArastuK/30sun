Why Customers Panic After Paying ₹60,000+

What causes post-booking anxiety, where it peaks, and one product intervention to fix it.

Arastu April 2026

Research

I went through the 30 Sundays funnel as a real customer before writing any of this.

  • 01 Submitted a trip inquiry on the website for Vietnam, 2 adults, 5 nights
  • 02 Received 3 curated itineraries via WhatsApp within 60 seconds from the AI bot
  • 03 Got assigned a dedicated consultant, call scheduled within 15 minutes
  • 04 Had a full consultation call covering pricing, visa process, and payment structure
  • 05 Read 30+ customer reviews on Holidify, App Store, and Google Reviews
  • 06 Mapped the post-booking journey against real customer complaints

Pre-sale experience

Bot replies in seconds. Consultant calls in 15 minutes. Quick, responsive, personal.

The product

Curated couple trips to 7 international destinations. ₹1-3L per couple. Consultant-driven, end-to-end planning.

Payment structure

100% of flight costs + 10% of landing costs upfront. For a typical Vietnam trip, that's ₹60,000-85,000 on day one.

Post-sale experience

Communication slows down significantly after payment. Customers lose visibility into what's happening with their trip. Reviews confirm this consistently.

Where Anxiety Lives

There are four distinct anxiety peaks after booking. In all of them, the customer has no way to tell if things are progressing normally or if something has gone wrong.

The Post-Booking Journey

Based on the funnel walkthrough, consultant call, and review analysis.

Day 0: Quote Received
Excitement and trust are both high
Consultant sends a detailed quote over WhatsApp. The couple discusses, maybe compares with MakeMyTrip or YouTube vlogs. Consultant is responsive, answers questions quickly.
Day 1-2: Payment Decision
Sending ₹60,000+ to a company found on Instagram
The couple decides to go ahead. Payment link arrives. As they type card details, doubts surface: Is this a good deal? What's the cancellation policy? What if the visa gets rejected? These are people used to paying ₹500 on Swiggy without thinking. ₹60K to a company they've known for 48 hours is a completely different decision.
The OTP moment is the first anxiety peak. Finger hovering over submit, sending ₹60K+ to a company with no physical office they've visited.
Day 3-10: The Void
Money is gone. Now what?
The consultant who was calling within 15 minutes pre-sale is now working on bookings behind the scenes. The customer sees none of it. No hotel confirmations, no timeline, no updates. Just silence.
"Before payment, they are extremely sweet and constantly follow up. But once the payment is done, their response becomes very poor. Calls are rarely answered and messages are slow."— Bharati, Mumbai · Holidify · 3 weeks ago
Day 10-20: Visa Processing
The thing that can cancel the entire trip
Consultant asks for passport copies and photos. Documents get submitted, then silence again. The customer has no idea if the visa has been submitted, is being processed, or is stuck. If it's rejected, they lose the trip and potentially the money.
Silence during visa processing could mean "on track" or "something went wrong." The customer has no way to know.
Day 20-28: Pre-Departure
Do I have everything?
Trip is days away. Vouchers aren't in one place, driver's name is unknown, airport transfer details are unclear. Everything is scattered across 3 weeks of WhatsApp messages.
"No ground support, no planning. They will promise Apple and give you lemon."— Vijay Pal, Pune · Holidify · 2 weeks ago
Day 29: Departure
Is someone actually picking us up?
At the airport with no driver name, no phone number, no confirmation that the transfer is arranged. What should be excitement is just more uncertainty.

Core Insight

The pre-sale experience is fast, proactive, and personal. The post-sale experience goes quiet. The team is still working, but the customer can't see any of it.

Every "any update?" message is a customer asking the same thing: "Is anyone actually working on my trip?"

The Pattern

The negative reviews aren't about trip quality, hotels, or pricing. They're all about the same thing: communication drops after payment.

The service isn't bad. It's invisible.
