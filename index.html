2. The solution

What it is

An AI travel assistant on WhatsApp that becomes the customer's source of truth for their trip. It captures every booking the consultant makes, automatically generates customer-facing updates, and answers any trip question 24/7 grounded in real data.

It's not a replacement for the consultant. It's the layer that makes the consultant's work visible, instant, and queryable.

How it works (the architecture)

The whole thing runs on one simple loop: forward → parse → store → notify.

StepWhat happens
1. ForwardConsultant forwards every booking confirmation (hotel, flight, visa, transfer) to a dedicated agent inbox or drops it in a channel the agent monitors. No new behavior, just one click on emails they already receive.
2. ParseLLM extracts structured fields: customer match, vendor, dates, room/seat type, price, confirmation number, voucher PDF.
3. StoreData goes into a structured trip record linked to the customer. Voucher gets attached automatically.
4. NotifyAgent generates a customer-facing WhatsApp message in 30 Sundays' brand voice and sends it instantly. The trip dashboard updates in parallel.

That's the entire system. No CRM rebuild, no API integrations with hotels and airlines, no behavior change for ops. Just inbox + LLM + WhatsApp Business API + database.

What the customer experiences

Pre-trip — fills the void. Every time the consultant books something, the customer gets a real-time update with details, photos, and the voucher attached. No more "is anyone working on my trip?" The customer can see the trip taking shape in real time.

What the consultant experiences

Almost nothing changes. They still book on supplier sites. They still own the customer relationship. The only new thing: they forward each confirmation email to the agent. One click.

What goes away:

  • Manually writing customer update messages after every booking
  • Hunting for vouchers in old emails three weeks later
  • Answering "any update?" messages five times a day
  • Being the sole memory of what was promised vs what was booked

Net effect: consultants spend less time on comms and admin, more time on planning, problem-solving, and customer relationships. The work that actually justifies a consultant-driven model.

Why this and not alternatives

AlternativeWhy it loses
"Train consultants to send proactive updates"Process fixes decay under load. Consultants forget, write inconsistent messages, and the data still isn't structured. The agent is the structure.
"Build a better post-booking app"The app already exists and adoption is low (28 Google Play reviews, 1K downloads). Customers live on WhatsApp. The agent meets them there.
"Add a status dashboard to the website"Same problem as the app. Customers won't pull. The agent pushes to where they already are.
"Integrate directly with hotel and airline APIs"Long, expensive, unreliable. Email forwarding is 10% of the work for 90% of the value. APIs become a v2 enhancement.
"Hire a 24/7 support team"Doesn't scale economically. AI handles the 80% of repetitive questions; humans handle the 20% that need judgment.

What this doesn't solve (honest trade-offs)

  • Verbal commitments. If a consultant promises something on a call ("I'll throw in a free spa session"), there's no email to forward. We'd need a "log a note" interface for these. Known gap.
  • Real-time external changes. If a hotel cancels at 2 AM, the agent doesn't know until someone tells it. v2 would integrate with supplier systems.
  • Customer match accuracy. The agent has to figure out which trip a forwarded confirmation belongs to. Solvable with name + date matching, but needs a "confirm this match?" prompt to the consultant when uncertain.
  • Adoption depends on consultant behavior. If consultants don't forward, the agent has nothing to work with. Mitigation: forwarding has to be easier than the current state. If it isn't, redesign until it is.
  • The agent must never bullshit. If data is missing, it says "I don't have that yet, here's why" and escalates. It never says "let me check and get back to you." This is a system design constraint.

Why this fits 30 Sundays specifically

  • 30 Sundays is already AI-first. Annie the pre-sale bot proves the team is comfortable building with AI.
  • WhatsApp is already the primary channel for both pre-sale and consultants. The agent extends what's working.
  • The team is small and ships fast. This is buildable in weeks, not quarters.
  • The biggest competitive risk in this category is trust erosion. Solving the post-payment communication gap protects every other investment 30 Sundays makes.
