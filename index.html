Reducing Post-Booking Anxiety at 30 Sundays

Arastu · April 2026
Problem statement: Customers book international trips worth ₹1-3L with 30 Sundays. After paying, many feel anxious about next steps: documents, visas, timelines. They contact support frequently, ops responds manually, increasing support load and reducing confidence.

Part 1. What causes anxiety and where it peaks

Research

I went through the 30 Sundays funnel as a customer before writing this:

  1. Submitted a trip inquiry on the website (Vietnam, 2 adults, 5 nights)
  2. Received 3 curated itineraries via WhatsApp within 60 seconds from the AI bot
  3. Got assigned a consultant, call scheduled within 15 minutes
  4. Had a consultation call covering pricing, visa process, payment structure
  5. Read 30+ customer reviews on Holidify, App Store, and Google Reviews

Key findings

AreaFinding
Pre-saleBot replies in seconds. Consultant calls in 15 minutes. Fast, responsive, personal.
Payment100% of flights + 10% of landing costs upfront. ₹60,000-85,000 on day one for a typical trip.
Post-saleCommunication slows down significantly. Customers lose visibility into what's happening with their trip.
Existing toolsPost-booking app exists (itinerary, doc vault, chat) but adoption is very low (12 App Store ratings). Consultant didn't mention it on the call.

Where anxiety peaks

Four points post-booking where anxiety spikes. In all of them, the customer can't tell if things are progressing normally or if something went wrong.

WhenWhat happensAnxiety
Day 1-2 Payment. ₹60K+ to a company found on Instagram 48 hours ago. Doubts about cancellation policy, visa risk, pricing. High
Day 3-10 The Void. Consultant is booking hotels and flights behind the scenes. Customer sees nothing. No confirmations, no timeline. Peak
Day 10-20 Visa processing. Documents submitted, then silence. If rejected, customer loses the trip and potentially the money. Peak
Day 20-28 Pre-departure. Vouchers, driver details, transfer info buried across 3 weeks of WhatsApp messages. High
Core insight: The pre-sale experience is fast, proactive, and personal. After payment, it goes quiet. The team is still working, but the customer can't see any of it. Every "any update?" message is someone asking: "Is anyone working on my trip?"
