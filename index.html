The Setup

Before solving the problem, I went through the 30 Sundays funnel as a real customer.

  • 02 Received 3 curated itineraries via WhatsApp within 60 seconds (AI bot "Annie")
  • 03 Assigned a dedicated consultant (Krishna) — call scheduled within 15 minutes
  • 04 Had a full consultation call — discussed pricing, visa process, payment structure
  • 05 Analyzed 30+ customer reviews on Holidify, App Store, and Google Reviews
  • 06 Mapped the complete post-booking journey against real customer complaints

Pre-sale experience

Fast, warm, responsive. Bot replies in seconds. Consultant calls in 15 minutes. The customer feels taken care of.

The product

Curated couple trips to 7 destinations. ₹1–3L per couple. High-touch, consultant-driven. Premium positioning.

Payment structure

100% of flight costs + 10% of landing costs upfront. For a typical Vietnam trip, that's ₹60,000–85,000 on day one.

The gap

The post-payment experience doesn't match the pre-payment promise. Customers enter a communication void — and reviews confirm it.

Where Anxiety Lives

The post-booking journey has four distinct anxiety peaks. The root cause is the same for all of them: the customer can't tell the difference between "things are progressing normally" and "things have gone wrong."

The Journey, Step by Step

Mapped from real customer behavior, consultant conversations, and review analysis.

Day 0 — Quote Received
Excitement is high. Trust is high.
The consultant sends a detailed quote over WhatsApp. The couple discusses it, maybe compares with MakeMyTrip or YouTube vlogs. The consultant is responsive, answers questions quickly. Everything feels right.
Day 1–2 — Payment Decision
₹60,000+ to a company found on Instagram
The couple decides to go ahead. A payment link arrives. As they type card details, every doubt surfaces: Is this a good deal? What's the cancellation policy? What if the visa gets rejected? They're used to paying ₹500 on Swiggy without thinking. This is different — this is a trust leap.
The OTP moment is the first anxiety peak. Finger hovering over "submit," sending ₹60K+ to a company they've known for 48 hours.
Day 3–10 — The Void
Money is gone. Now what?
This is where the experience breaks. The consultant — who was calling within 15 minutes pre-sale — is now working on bookings behind the scenes. But the customer sees none of it. No hotel confirmations. No timeline. No "here's what's happening." Just silence.
"Before payment, they are extremely sweet and constantly follow up. But once the payment is done, their response becomes very poor. Calls are rarely answered and messages are slow."— Bharati, Mumbai · Holidify · 3 weeks ago
Day 10–20 — Visa Black Box
The one thing that can cancel the entire trip
The consultant asks for passport copies and photos. Documents are submitted — and then silence again. The customer has no idea if the visa has been submitted, is being processed, or is stuck. This is the highest-stakes unknown: if the visa is rejected, they lose the trip and potentially the money.
The customer can't distinguish between "processing normally" and "something went wrong." Silence means both.
Day 20–28 — Pre-Departure Scramble
Do I have everything?
The trip is days away. The customer doesn't have vouchers in one place, doesn't know their driver's name, isn't sure about airport transfers. Information is scattered across 3 weeks of WhatsApp messages. They start asking: "Can you send everything in one message?"
"No ground support, no planning. They will promise Apple and give you lemon."— Vijay Pal, Pune · Holidify · 2 weeks ago
Day 29 — Departure
Is someone actually picking us up?
At the airport, one question remains: "Will the transfer actually be there?" The customer doesn't have the driver's number. The experience that should be pure excitement is laced with uncertainty.

The Core Insight

The pre-sale experience sets an expectation of speed, warmth, and proactivity. The post-sale experience breaks that promise — not because the team isn't working, but because the work is invisible to the customer.

Every support call, every "any update?" message, every anxious follow-up is a customer saying the same thing: "I can't tell if you're working on my trip."

The Pattern

Across every negative review, the complaint isn't about trip quality, hotel standards, or pricing. It's about the same thing: communication drops after payment.

The anxiety isn't caused by bad service. It's caused by invisible service.
